Biot Savart Law

For a circular current loop, the magnetic field of a current element dI can be found first by looking at the infinitesimal magnetic field element 'dB' generated due to the current: $$ d\mathbf{B} = \frac{{\mu}_oI}{4\pi} \times \frac{d\mathbf{I} \times \mathbf{r}}{r^3}$$ To calculate the total magnetic field at the point P we must integrate the infinitesimal magnetic field element across the entire loop: $$\mathbf{B} = \frac{{\mu}_oI}{4\pi} \int_{C}\frac{d\mathbf{I} \times \mathbf{r}}{r^2} $$
